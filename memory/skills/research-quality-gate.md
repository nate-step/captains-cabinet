# Skill: Research Quality Gate

**Status:** promoted
**Created by:** foundation
**Date:** 2026-03-30
**Validated against:** research brief review, actionability assessment
**Usage count:** 0

## When to Use

Every time CRO completes a research sweep or produces a research brief, before publishing and notifying other Officers.

## Procedure

Before publishing a research brief, verify all 4 checks pass:

1. **So what?** Every finding must answer: "What should the product team do differently because of this?" If a finding doesn't change any decision or action, cut it.

2. **Concrete recommendations.** Every brief must end with a "Recommendations" section containing specific, actionable items addressed to specific Officers (e.g., "CPO: prioritize audio notes — 3 of 5 competitors launched this in Q1").

3. **Evidence quality.** Distinguish between verified facts (product launched, pricing confirmed) and inferences (market trend, user sentiment). Label your confidence level.

4. **Relevance filter.** Does this research connect to current sprint priorities, Captain directives, or identified strategic gaps? Research that doesn't connect to anything the Cabinet is working on has low value.

## Expected Outcome

Every published brief has: actionable findings that change decisions, concrete recommendations addressed to specific Officers, clearly labeled evidence quality, and connection to current work priorities.

## Known Pitfalls

- Research briefs that read like Wikipedia articles — informative but not actionable
- Findings without "so what" that nobody acts on
- Mixing verified facts with speculation without labeling the difference
- Researching topics nobody is working on while current sprint questions go unanswered

## Validation Scenarios

- Scenario 1: CRO writes brief → every finding has a "so what" → CPO changes backlog priority based on it
- Scenario 2: CRO discovers competitor → recommendation is specific enough for CPO to create a Linear issue
- Scenario 3: Brief distinguishes "confirmed pricing" from "estimated market size" with confidence labels
